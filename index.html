Analysis of Marketing Campaign Data for Term Deposit Subscriptions

I completed a data analysis project using SQL to explore a bank marketing dataset. The dataset contained data on direct marketing campaigns carried out by a Portuguese banking institution, including information about the campaign, the client, and the outcomes of the marketing effort. With 20 attributes and over 41,000 records, I aimed to answer three key questions: (1) Analyzing contact methods for lead conversion to subscribers, (2) Analyzing customer demographics and their relation to campaign success, and (3) Analyzing the relationship between contact type and success. To answer these questions, I used SQL to clean and preprocess the data, and then performed various queries and visualizations to analyze the results. The findings from this analysis could help banking institutions make more informed decisions on their marketing campaigns and strategies. The project was a valuable learning experience in data analysis, providing me with a deeper understanding of the potential insights that can be gained from data-driven decision-making.

Exploring the Impact of Weather on Bike Rentals in Washington D.C.

In this project, I set out to analyze the impact of weather conditions on bike rentals in a specific city. My research question was how different weather conditions affect the number of bike rentals, with the hypothesis being that there is a correlation between weather conditions and rental numbers. The implications of the findings could be significant for bike-sharing companies, transportation planners, and city governments, as understanding the relationship can help plan and allocate resources to promote bike-sharing programs.
